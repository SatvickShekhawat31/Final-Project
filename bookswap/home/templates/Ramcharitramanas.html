Book Details


Shri Ramcharitmanas is a 16th-century Hindu epic poem written by Tulsidas. The poem is a retelling of the Sanskrit epic Ramayana written by Valmiki 2,500 years ago. It is written in Awadhi, a dialect similar to Hindi. The word Ramcharitmanas translates to "Lake of the deeds of Rama". The poem is known for its expression of love for a personal god and its portrayal of ideal conduct for a husband, wife, and brother. The poem is also known as Tulsi Ramayana, Tulsikrit Ramayana, Tulsidas Ramayana, or simply Manas. The poem is over 12,000 lines long and has over 116,000 words. Some say that the entire poem should be read continuously for 24 hours.
